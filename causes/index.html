We've partnered with Pepin Academies for September, read more below

Pepin Academies is a 501(c)(3) not-for-profit charter school that provides tuition free education to students with identified learning and learning related disabilities. Our first encounter with Pepin was at their Tampa campus, where Charles and I had a chance to meet with one of the school’s founders, Crisha Murray-Scolaro, who taught us the difficulties students with specific learning disabilities (SDL) often face throughout their school careers. After learning about the long term solutions Pepin Academies provides their students, we knew we had to get involved.

Most kids with SDL struggle through schools that often don’t have the resources or full time programs to cater to their needs. Those that are lucky enough to find schools that do, eventually graduate out as they move on to middle and high school. In this environment, these kids are often susceptible to bullying, and with the schools lack of resources, their education can leave them unprepared for life after classes.

For over fifteen years, Pepin Academies has helped hundreds of students with SDL graduate high school, go to college, and find jobs. All while spending their school careers in a safe, innovative learning environment. At Spark, we’re all about using technology to create long term solutions, which is why for the month of September, we’ve set our sites on empower these students with enhanced classroom technology, so this great school can continue to change the lives of their students.
